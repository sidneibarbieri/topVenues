The six labels (read before you start)
  • valid — a complete, on-topic abstract of the right paper, with nothing extraneous.
  • truncated — part of the text is missing, whether at the beginning, the middle, or the end.
  • contaminated — the right abstract, with extra text attached: author names, affiliations, references, or navigation.
  • wrong_paper — the text belongs to a different paper.
  • not_abstract — the field holds a title, an author list, or other metadata rather than an abstract.
  • uncertain — the text and title alone do not settle it.

After choosing truncated, contaminated, wrong_paper, or not_abstract, paste the correct abstract into the field that appears. It becomes the repair recorded for a later release.
